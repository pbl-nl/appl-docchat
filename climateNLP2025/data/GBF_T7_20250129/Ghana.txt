National target title: Enhance access to relevant and reliable data for decision-making

Description: Enhance access to relevant and reliable data to policy makers, practitioners and the public to guide effective governance and participatory management of biodiversity, and to strengthen communication, awareness-raising, education, monitoring, research and knowledge management and traditional knowledge and practices

Main policy measures: Establish a national data management system for biodiversity information Promote public participation and access to biodiversity information Encourage citizen science and traditional knowledge-generated data to complement conventional data collection methods (census, surveys and administrative data) Promote awareness creation and engagement on biodiversity data for decision making

National target title: Ensure the full integration of biodiversity and its multiple values into policies, regulations, development planning  and budgetary processes

Description: Ensure the full integration of biodiversity and its multiple values into policies, regulations, development planning processes and strategies, environmental assessments, and Natural Capital Accounting to progressively align all relevant public and private activities, fiscal and financial flows with the goals and targets of this framework

Main policy measures: Mainstream biodiversity into the National Development Planning, Budgeting, Monitoring and Evaluation processes, National Development Policy Framework, and Medium-Term Development Plan for MDAs and MMDAs using tools like SEA, EIA, among others Adopt the System of Environmental-Economic Accounting (SEEA)  framework to produce  NCA   to inform biodiversity-inclusive   decision-making processes Build the capacity of MDAs, MMDAs, private sectors and relevant stakeholders in mainstreaming biodiversity in the National Plans

National target title: Reduce the risk and the negative impacts of pollution from all sources to levels that are not harmful to biodiversity

Description: By 2030, reduce the risk and the negative impacts of pollution from all sources to levels that are not harmful to biodiversity and ecosystem functions and services especially from   the loss of excess nutrients to the environment by at least half and the overall risk from pesticides and highly hazardous chemicals by at least half while preventing, reducing, and working towards eliminating plastic pollution

Main policy measures: Sensitize and create awareness of the harmful effects of pesticides and hazardous chemicals Update standards, guidelines and regulations on the use of all pesticides  and chemicals Enhance enforcement of protocols on the use of all pesticides  and chemicals Enhance the implementation of the provisions within the National Plastic Policy Mainstream the National Plastic Policy into the Medium-Term  National Development Plan (MTNDP)  and the National Environmental  Sanitation Strategy and Action Plan (NESSAP) Create awareness on plastic pollution reduction measures and promote biodegradable options
